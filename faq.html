Getting started

The basics

What is CartChkr? #

CartChkr is a free Chrome extension for Australian grocery shoppers. You load up your trolley at Woolies or Coles as normal, click the CartChkr button, and it compares every item against the same product at the other store. Anything cheaper at the other store gets a one-click Move button that switches the item across for you.

It works in both directions — start at Woolies and compare to Coles, or start at Coles and compare to Woolies.

Which browsers does it work on? #

Chrome and any Chromium-based browser — Microsoft Edge, Brave, Arc, Opera, Vivaldi. If you can install extensions from the Chrome Web Store, CartChkr works.

It does not work on Safari, Firefox, or mobile browsers (Chrome for iOS/Android don't support extensions). Firefox is on the roadmap; Safari and mobile are further out.

Do I need to sign up for a CartChkr account? #

No account, no email signup. CartChkr uses your existing Woolworths and Coles logins — it reads the prices your browser already has access to and never asks for your credentials.

How do I install it? #

Open the CartChkr Chrome Web Store page and click Add to Chrome. After installing, pin CartChkr to your toolbar (click the puzzle-piece icon, then the pin next to CartChkr) so it's easy to find. Open Woolies or Coles, click the CartChkr icon, and the sidepanel will appear.

Why does it ask to "read data on woolworths.com.au and coles.com.au"? #

To compare prices, CartChkr needs to read your trolley contents and look up the matching items at the other store. That's all the permission is for. It can't see anything on any other site, it never reads your passwords or payment details, and it doesn't send your cart anywhere — see the privacy section.

Using it

Scanning, matching, moving

How do I scan my cart? #

Sign into both stores in separate tabs and load up your trolley at one of them. Open the CartChkr sidepanel, then click the W (Scan) button if your trolley is at Woolies, or the C (Scan) button if it's at Coles. CartChkr fetches every item, finds the matching product at the other store, and shows you a side-by-side comparison.

Typical scan takes 5-15 seconds depending on cart size. You'll see a progress bar.

Why are some items marked "no match"? #

Three common reasons:

  • Private label products — Woolworths own-brand items (Macro, Essentials, etc.) usually don't have an exact Coles equivalent, and the same the other way around.
  • Fresh produce PLU codes — Woolies and Coles use different internal codes for things like loose capsicums or bananas, so an exact-code search returns nothing. CartChkr tries a name-based fallback for these but it doesn't always find a confident match.
  • Genuinely store-exclusive items — some lines are only stocked at one chain.

If you spot a "no match" that you think IS available, click the 👎 on the match — we use those signals to improve scoring.

How does the Move button actually work? #

Click Move and CartChkr adds the matching product to your trolley at the cheaper store, then removes the item from your original cart. If anything fails partway through, it rolls back automatically so you don't end up with an item missing or duplicated.

Everything happens inside your own browser session using your existing logins — no separate checkout, no payment to us beyond the one-time licence.

Can I undo a Move? #

If a Move fails for any reason, CartChkr rolls it back automatically — the item goes back to its original cart. For a successful Move you've changed your mind about: just remove the item from the new cart and re-add it at the original store. We'd love to add a one-click undo down the track but it's not in v1.

Can CartChkr compare ALDI, IGA, or Costco too? #

Not yet. CartChkr currently compares Woolworths and Coles — the two stores with comparable online trolley experiences. ALDI doesn't offer the same online cart functionality, and IGA / Costco vary store by store. We're aiming at New Zealand next (Pak'nSave + Woolworths NZ), then revisiting AU options.

Does this work on mobile or iPad? #

No — CartChkr is a Chrome desktop extension. Chrome on iOS and Android doesn't support extensions, and Safari is a separate platform. Mobile support is on the long-term roadmap.

Does CartChkr work with Everyday Rewards / Flybuys pricing? #

Yes. As long as you're signed in at Woolworths with your Everyday Rewards account (and Coles with your Flybuys account), CartChkr sees the prices you actually pay — member specials and reward pricing are baked into the comparison.

Pricing & accounts

What it costs, how to manage it

Is CartChkr free? #

Scanning is free, forever. Add items to your Woolies or Coles cart, hit Scan, see the comparison — no payment.

The Move feature (one-click switch to the cheaper store) requires a one-time licence:

  • Founding Member — 99¢ one-time, first 500 users only
  • Lifetime — $3.99 one-time, full access forever
  • Monthly — $2.99/month, full access plus early access to new stores

Most users save $15-$25 per shop, so the licence pays for itself the first time you use it.

How do I upgrade to unlock Move? #

Click any Move button in the sidepanel. You'll see a plan picker — choose Founding, Lifetime, or Monthly — and complete checkout through ExtensionPay (which handles billing via Stripe). Your plan activates the moment payment succeeds.

Can I get a refund? #

Yes — email support@cartchkr.com.au within 14 days of payment and we'll refund you, no questions asked. Refunds process through Stripe and usually land in your account within 5-10 business days.

I cleared my browser data — did I lose my licence? #

No. Your plan is tied to your ExtensionPay account, not your browser. Open CartChkr → click the ⚙ icon → Sign in / restore purchase. Use the same email you bought with and your plan will be restored.

How do I cancel my monthly subscription? #

Open CartChkr → click the ⚙ icon → Manage subscription. This opens your ExtensionPay billing portal where you can cancel anytime. Your access continues until the end of the current billing period.

If you have trouble, email support@cartchkr.com.au and we'll sort it out.

I paid but the Move feature is still locked — what do I do? #

Close the CartChkr sidepanel completely (click the toolbar icon to dismiss), then reopen it. The licence check runs fresh on every open and should activate immediately.

If it's still locked after reopening, email support@cartchkr.com.au with your payment confirmation email and we'll manually verify and activate your account.

Privacy & data

What we see and don't see

What data does CartChkr collect? #

Your cart contents stay in your browser. CartChkr does not send your shopping data to any server we control. It doesn't store what you buy, when you shop, or what your trolley looks like.

The only external service it touches is ExtensionPay (via Stripe) for licence verification, if you've bought a plan. ExtensionPay sees your email and payment status — nothing about your shopping. See our Privacy Policy for the full breakdown.

Do you see my Woolies or Coles login details? #

No. CartChkr never reads, stores, or transmits your passwords or auth tokens. It uses the active session your browser already has with each store — exactly the same session you use to shop normally.

What about GDPR / opt-out / deleting my data? #

There's no telemetry to opt out of — we don't collect any. If you've bought a plan and want your ExtensionPay account data deleted, email support@cartchkr.com.au and we'll process the deletion through ExtensionPay's customer-data flow within 30 days.

Is CartChkr affiliated with Woolworths or Coles? #

No. CartChkr is an independent tool built by an Australian developer. It's not affiliated with, endorsed by, or sponsored by Woolworths Group Limited or Coles Group Limited. We just shop there like everyone else and got tired of comparing prices by hand.

Troubleshooting

When something's not working

Why doesn't Coles load? / Coles tab keeps showing a loading screen #

You've hit Coles' bot-detection (Imperva). It's not CartChkr being blocked specifically — Coles runs a security check that occasionally challenges automated traffic, including the kind of programmatic browsing the extension does. Fix:

  1. Switch to your Coles tab in Chrome.
  2. Wait 5-10 seconds for the loading screen to finish — Imperva runs a JavaScript challenge and sets a cookie.
  3. Come back to the CartChkr sidepanel and hit Scan again.

If it keeps happening every shop: sign out and back in at coles.com.au — that usually clears any stuck session state. We're working on a fully automated workaround but for now this manual nudge is the most reliable fix.

My Woolies session expired / "Please sign in" loop #

Woolworths sessions last about 24 hours. If yours has expired, sign back in at woolworths.com.au, then retry the scan. CartChkr will pick up the fresh session automatically.

The extension found the wrong match for one of my items #

Click the 👎 next to the bad match in the sidepanel. CartChkr will block that pairing for future scans and we use those signals to improve the matcher. You can also click 👍 on a correct match to lock it in — saved matches persist across scans.

Common causes of bad matches: branded vs unbranded confusion (e.g. Cadbury vs Coles-brand chocolate), size variants (250g vs 500g), and fresh produce naming differences. We enforce brand-match for third-party brands but the fresh-produce side is still imperfect.

I clicked Move but nothing happened / item didn't appear in the new cart #

Most often this is the same Coles bot-block issue — the Move couldn't complete because Coles was rate-limiting. Follow the steps in the Coles loading question, then click Move again.

If it's not bot-block: CartChkr has a name-based fallback for fresh produce that sometimes needs a second pass. Clicking Move a second time will often succeed.

If it still fails after two tries, click Report Issue in the sidepanel footer — diagnostic logs are attached automatically and we'll investigate.

I see "No matches found" on every item #

If literally every item shows no match, the target store is bot-blocking your whole session. Manually visit coles.com.au or woolworths.com.au, complete any "Verify you're human" check that appears, then come back and re-scan.

How do I report a bug? #

Use the Report Issue button in the CartChkr sidepanel footer. You'll get three options: open Gmail in browser, open your default mail app, or copy the diagnostic logs to clipboard. Whichever you pick, the logs (cart state, last few errors, extension version) are attached automatically — you don't need to write anything technical, just describe what you were trying to do.

Where can I see my saved or rejected matches? #

Open the CartChkr sidepanel → click the Saved tab at the top. You'll see every match you've thumbs-upped (saved) and thumbs-downed (rejected). From there you can unpin a saved match or unblock a rejected one.

Why does the same item sometimes cost more at Woolies one week and less the next? #

Woolies and Coles run weekly specials that rotate constantly — hundreds of items change price every week depending on supplier deals, promotional calendars, and competitive pricing. There's no consistent pattern; the cheaper store genuinely changes week to week for most households.

That's exactly why CartChkr exists. Checking by hand takes 20+ minutes. CartChkr does it in 10 seconds.
