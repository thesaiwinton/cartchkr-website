Knowledge Base

KB-001 User-side Extension shows nothing / blank panel
▼

Side panel opens but is empty, or shows a spinner that never completes.

  1. Close and reopen the CartChkr side panel — click the toolbar icon to close it, then click again to reopen.
  2. If that doesn't help: reload your Woolies or Coles tab (Ctrl+R on PC / Cmd+R on Mac), then reopen the panel.
  3. Still blank? Go to chrome://extensions → find CartChkr → click the circular refresh arrow to reload the extension.
This usually happens just after installing or updating CartChkr. Chrome needs a page reload before the extension can connect to it.
KB-002 Intermittent Wrong price shown for an item
▼

CartChkr shows a price that doesn't match what you see on the store website.

  1. Check if the item is a multi-pack (e.g. 3-pack of yoghurt). CartChkr normalises to a per-unit price for a fair comparison — the price shown may be per-unit, not per-pack.
  2. Reload your Woolies or Coles cart tab and scan again. Specials update weekly and there can be a brief lag.
  3. If the price is still wrong, report it with: the item name, the price CartChkr shows, the actual price on the website, and your diagnostic logs (click ⚙ in CartChkr → Copy Logs).
A known issue with Woolworths sale price data was fixed in v0.2.0. Update your extension if you're on an older version.
KB-003 Known limitation Item shows "no match" but it exists at the other store
▼

An item shows the "no match" badge even though you can find it at the other store manually.

  1. Click the Search link next to the "no match" item. This opens a pre-filled search on the other store so you can find it manually.
  2. If you find the right product, click ✓ to pin that match — CartChkr will remember it for every future scan.
  3. If the store was recently bot-blocked (you'll see a warning during the scan), wait 5–10 minutes and scan again.
Some products have very different names between stores (e.g. Woolworths Select vs Coles brand). CartChkr will improve matching over time — pinning correct matches helps.
KB-004 User-side Wrong product matched (different brand or size)
▼

CartChkr matched your item to a different brand or wrong size at the other store.

  1. Click the ✕ button on the item card to reject the wrong match. CartChkr will search for better alternatives.
  2. If a better match appears in the results, click ✓ to pin it. CartChkr will remember this for every future scan.
  3. If there's no good equivalent at the other store, the item will show as "no match" — which is the most accurate result.
KB-005 Intermittent Move button doesn't work / item not added
▼

Clicked Move (→C or →W), button spun, but item didn't appear in the other store's cart.

  1. Make sure you're logged in to both Woolies and Coles. CartChkr needs an active session on both stores.
  2. Have both store cart pages open in separate tabs before scanning.
  3. If you see a bot-block warning: open the target store's website, browse normally for about 30 seconds (add something to cart, scroll through products), then try Move again.
  4. Check that the item is actually in stock at the target store.
The Move feature requires a paid licence. If you're seeing the upgrade prompt, scan is free — Move is the paid feature.
KB-006 Intermittent Scan failed or returned no results
▼

Scan starts but stops partway, shows an error, or completes with 0 results.

  1. Make sure you're on the cart/trolley page, not the home page or a product page.
    • Woolies cart: woolworths.com.au/cart
    • Coles trolley: coles.com.au/trolley
  2. Confirm you're logged in to the store you're scanning and have at least 1 item in your cart.
  3. If it's a bot-block (you'll see a warning in the panel): wait 5–10 minutes, reload the store page, and scan again.
KB-007 User-side Can't find CartChkr after installing
▼

CartChkr is installed but you can't see it in Chrome's toolbar.

  1. Click the puzzle piece icon 🧩 in Chrome's toolbar (top right).
  2. Find CartChkr in the list and click the pin icon to keep it visible.
  3. Navigate to a Woolies or Coles page, then click the CartChkr icon. The side panel will open on the right side of your browser.
CartChkr only activates on woolworths.com.au and coles.com.au pages. The icon may appear greyed out on other sites — this is normal.
KB-008 Expected behaviour Prices changed after logging in with Everyday Rewards
▼

Prices look different after logging in with an Everyday Rewards account.

  1. This is correct behaviour. CartChkr uses your actual logged-in prices, including Everyday Rewards member pricing which is often lower than the shelf price.
  2. Always make sure you're logged in to Woolworths with your Everyday Rewards account before scanning — this gives you the most accurate and personally relevant comparison.
Member specials are included automatically. The comparison you see reflects YOUR prices, not the general shelf price.
KB-009 Known limitation Bot-block warning shown during scan
▼

CartChkr shows a bot-block warning; some items can't be compared.

  1. This is temporary — Woolies and Coles have security systems that occasionally flag the comparison requests. It usually clears within 5–10 minutes.
  2. Browse the affected store's website normally for 1–2 minutes (scroll through products, add something to cart) to reset your session.
  3. Scan again. The block almost always clears on the next attempt.
  4. Scanning smaller carts (under 30 items) is less likely to trigger this.
KB-010 User-side Payment made but Move is still locked
▼

You paid but the Move button still shows the upgrade prompt.

  1. Close the CartChkr panel completely — click the ✕ or the toolbar icon to close it.
  2. Reopen CartChkr by clicking the toolbar icon again. The licence check runs fresh every time the panel opens.
  3. The Move feature should now be unlocked. If it's still showing the upgrade prompt after reopening, email support@cartchkr.com.au with your payment confirmation and we'll sort it out manually.

Still stuck?

If none of the above helped, email us and we'll get back to you with your own reference number within 24 hours.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is CartChkr free? ▼

Free Scanning is completely free. Add items to your Woolies or Coles cart, press Scan, and CartChkr will compare every item against the other store and show you which is cheaper — no payment required, ever.

Paid The Move feature — which transfers your whole cart to the cheaper store in one click — requires a licence. We offer three options:

  • Founding Member — 99¢ one-time (limited-time early adopter price)
  • Lifetime — $3.99 one-time, full access forever
  • Monthly — $2.99/month, full access + early access to new stores

Most users save $15–$25 per shop, so the licence pays for itself the first time you use it.

How does CartChkr compare prices? ▼

CartChkr reads your cart directly from the Woolworths or Coles website using your logged-in session. It then searches for each item at the other store and compares the live prices — including any member specials, Everyday Rewards pricing, or Coles member discounts you're entitled to.

Everything runs locally in your browser. CartChkr never sends your cart contents, login details, or personal information anywhere.

Does CartChkr work with Everyday Rewards? ▼

Yes. As long as you're logged in to Woolworths with your Everyday Rewards account before scanning, CartChkr will use your member pricing automatically. The comparison you see reflects your actual prices — not the general shelf price.

Is my data private? Does CartChkr collect anything? ▼

CartChkr does not collect, store, or transmit your cart contents, passwords, payment details, or any personal information. Everything runs locally in your browser.

The only external service CartChkr contacts is ExtensionPay (via Stripe) to verify your licence status if you've purchased one. See our Privacy Policy for full details.

Why does the same item sometimes cost more at Woolies one week and less the next? ▼

Woolworths and Coles run weekly specials that rotate constantly. Hundreds of items change price every week depending on supplier deals, promotional calendars, and competition between the stores. There's no consistent pattern — the cheaper store genuinely changes week to week for most households.

That's exactly why CartChkr exists: checking manually takes 20+ minutes. CartChkr does it in 10 seconds.

Does CartChkr work on mobile or iPad? ▼

CartChkr is a Chrome desktop extension and works on Windows and Mac computers. Chrome extensions are not supported on Chrome for iOS or Android, so CartChkr does not work on mobile browsers.

Mobile support is on our roadmap — follow cartchkr.com.au for updates.

Can CartChkr compare ALDI prices too? ▼

Not yet. CartChkr currently compares Woolworths and Coles. ALDI doesn't have a comparable online shopping experience with cart functionality, which makes comparison technically different. It's on the roadmap.

How do I cancel my monthly subscription? ▼

Open CartChkr → click the ⚙ icon → Manage subscription. This opens your ExtensionPay billing portal where you can cancel anytime. Your access continues until the end of the billing period.

If you have trouble, email support@cartchkr.com.au and we'll sort it out.

I paid but the Move feature is still locked — what do I do? ▼

Close the CartChkr side panel completely (click the ✕ or toolbar icon), then reopen it. The licence check runs fresh every time the panel opens and should activate immediately.

If it's still locked after reopening, email support@cartchkr.com.au with your payment confirmation email and we'll manually verify and activate your account.

Is CartChkr affiliated with Woolworths or Coles? ▼

No. CartChkr is an independent tool built by an Australian developer. It is not affiliated with, endorsed by, or connected to Woolworths Group or Coles Group in any way.
